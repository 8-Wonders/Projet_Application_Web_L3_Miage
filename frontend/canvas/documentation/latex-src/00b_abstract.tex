\begin{abstract}
	\noindent
The abstract summarizes the central content of the work. No evaluation or interpretation is included.
This helps to get a rough overview of the research question, approach, and results.
It should include the parts a) background information, research question, objective, research context, b) methods, c) results, and d) conclusions, application possibilities.
The text is concise, complete, and precise, as well as objective and without personal evaluation.
Pay attention to simple and understandable language. All mentioned content must also be addressed in the main part.
Reproduce the content objectively and without personal evaluation. Address the most important concepts, results, or conclusions.
Do not use citations and avoid abbreviations. As a rule, approx. 200 words are sufficient.
\end{abstract}
